Lesson 2: Linear Functions as Patterns and Rates

A sequence of numbers follows a specific linear pattern. The first term is 14, the second term is 21, and the third term is 28. Which of the following expressions represents the value of the $n$-th term in this sequence?

An employee's total accumulated paid time off (PTO), in hours, forms a linear pattern based on the number of full weeks worked. After 2 weeks, the employee has 11 hours of PTO. After 3 weeks, they have 14.5 hours of PTO. If this pattern continues, how many hours of PTO will the employee have accumulated after 4 weeks?

The table below shows partial values for the linear function $f$.

$x$ $f(x)$
$3$ $19$
$5$ $13$
$7$ $7$

If the pattern shown in the table continues at a constant rate, what is the value of $f(11)$?

A linear pattern is defined by a process where each subsequent term is generated by adding a constant value, $k$, to the preceding term. If the 4th term of this pattern is 23 and the 7th term is 41, what is the value of $k$?

Which of the following data tables represents a completely linear relationship between $x$ and $y$?

A manufacturing floor tracks historical production errors. The line supervisor determines that the number of defects drops according to a strict linear pattern for every hour the new calibration system runs. At hour 2, there are 84 defects. At hour 5, there are 66 defects. Which statement accurately describes the rate of this pattern?

A plant biologist records the daily height of a sunflower stalk. The stalk grows at a constant rate. If the stalk is 42 centimeters tall on day 8, and its height follows the pattern $H(d) = 3.5d + b$, where $d$ is the day number and $b$ is a constant, what was the height of the stalk, in centimeters, on day 3?

A sequence of stacked block shapes follows a linear pattern where the total number of blocks, $B_n$, in design step $n$ is given by a linear function. If design step 5 requires 38 blocks and design step 12 requires 87 blocks, how many blocks are required for design step 20?

Values for a linear function $g(x)$ are generated such that for every increase in $x$ by 1 unit, the value of $g(x)$ increases by a constant rate $r$. If $g(4) = k$ and $g(7) = k + 12$, what is the value of $g(10) - g(2)$?

A data analyst notes that the registration entries for a virtual conference grow according to a linear pattern. The table below represents the total registration entries, $E$, recorded $d$ days after the portal opened, where $x$ and $y$ are unknown constants.

Days($d$) Entries($E$)
$3$ $x$
$6$ $y$
$15$ $2y - 10$

If the rate of change of this pattern is exactly 15 entries per day, what is the value of the constant $x$?
